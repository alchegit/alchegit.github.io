Privacy Policy

Last Updated: May 26, 2026

Lucky Card Random Defense (package name: com.neokim.luckycarddefense, "the app") is a Godot-based Android 2D card/random defense game. This Privacy Policy explains what information the app processes and how that information is used.

1. Personal Information Collected Directly by the App

The current version of the app does not collect or sell personal information through a self-operated server that directly identifies a user, such as name, email address, phone number, contacts, photos, camera data, microphone data, or precise location information. The current version of the app does not provide a self-operated server, account registration, sign-in, chat, community, or cloud save feature.

The current build does not include the Google Mobile Ads SDK, Firebase, Google Play Games Services, a separate analytics SDK, in-app purchases, or subscription SDKs. Therefore, the current version of the app does not collect or transmit advertising ID, account information, payment information, or leaderboard scores by itself.

2. Game Data Processed on the Device

To run gameplay, the app may process game state in memory during app execution, such as current gold, wave, base HP, summoned units, merge state, selected card effects, defeated enemy count, and win/loss state. The current version of the app does not transmit this gameplay state to a self-operated server or a third-party server.

If future versions add features such as resume, settings storage, best records, or achievement progress, such data may be stored locally on the user's device to provide app features. If these features are actually added, this Privacy Policy and the Google Play Data safety declaration will be updated accordingly.

3. Advertising and Third-Party SDKs

The current public/test build does not include a real advertising SDK, analytics SDK, or leaderboard SDK. The current version of the app also does not request internet or network state permissions.

If the app includes Google AdMob or another advertising SDK in the future, the app may display banner ads or rewarded ads only when the user clearly chooses to view them. In that case, Google or its advertising partners may process information such as advertising ID, device information, app information, IP address, approximate location, ad impressions, and ad interaction information for ad serving, ads personalization, frequency capping, fraud prevention, ad measurement, and service improvement.

For more details about Google's advertising data practices, please see Google Advertising Privacy & Terms.

4. Android Permissions

The current version of the app does not request sensitive permissions such as camera, microphone, contacts, photos, precise location, notifications, or storage. The current Android export configuration also does not enable internet, location, camera, microphone, contacts, notification, or storage-related permissions.

If new permissions are needed due to future features or advertising SDK integration, this will be reflected in the app description, this Privacy Policy, and the Google Play Data safety declaration.

5. Purpose of Use

Game state processed inside the current app is used to provide gameplay features, including battle progress, unit summon, unit merge, wave progression, card reward selection, HP and gold display, and clear/failure result display.

If local storage features are added in the future, such information will be used only to provide app features such as resume, settings persistence, and record display. If an advertising SDK is actually added in the future, advertising-related information may be processed according to the SDK's purposes, such as ad serving, fraud prevention, and ad measurement.

6. Retention and Deletion

The current version of the app does not store user gameplay data on a self-operated server. Battle state held in memory during app execution may no longer be retained after the app or battle is closed.

If local storage features are added in the future, users can delete locally stored app data by clearing app data/storage in Android settings or by uninstalling the app. If an advertising SDK is actually introduced, retention and deletion of advertising-related information processed by Google or other third parties will be managed according to those services' privacy policies, ad settings, applicable laws, and the user's consent/choices.

7. Third-Party Sharing and International Processing

The current version of the app does not transmit or sell game records or settings to a self-operated server or a third-party server. Because the current build does not include a real advertising SDK, analytics SDK, or leaderboard SDK, no third-party sharing occurs through those SDKs.

If Google AdMob, Google Play Games Services, an analytics SDK, or another third-party SDK is actually added in the future, those service providers may process information needed to provide their services, and such information may be processed outside the user's country. If such a change occurs, this Privacy Policy will be updated.

8. Children's Privacy

The app does not knowingly collect personal information that directly identifies users, including children, such as name, contact information, or precise location information by itself. If advertising or account-based services are added in the future, Google Account, Google Play, and advertising service age settings, parental consent, visibility settings, ads personalization settings, and applicable laws may apply.
